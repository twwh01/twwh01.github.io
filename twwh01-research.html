Research

Overview

My research focuses on understanding Earth's environments and climate in deep-time, particularly during the Cambrian Period when many of the modern animal phyla first appear in the fossil record. I am particularly interested in combining quantitative and semi-quantitative geological data with numerical model simulations to try to constrain global conditions using real, local, data. This was the focus of my PhD research at the University of Leicester.

I am also interested in the fossil evidence of the animals (and other organisms) that made up the biosphere at this time. Here, I try to explore links between the physical environments these animals lived and died in, and the mechanisms behind the exceptional preservation of the fossils we discover in the rocks today. Again, I approach these questions by combining geological data collected in the field with numerical models of past environmetnal conditions. More on this to follow soon!
